Neuroscience & Economics

Overview

My name is Daniel Moldovan and I am currently an undergraduate student at UC Riverside where I am studying Neuroscience & Economics. My interest in Neuroscience combines two fields I am fascinated by, Biology and Psychology. Along my educational journey I also realized the importance of having a strong understanding of economics and finances, areas that can be widely applied across career paths. I still have a couple of quarters left before I finish both degree requirements and graduate but I already have a keen interest in working in healthcare administration while pursuing my Masters in Finance.

Journey

My path to UCR has been unique. I worked as an auto mechanic for many years in Portland, Oregon and Orange County, California before realizing that I needed a career and not just a job. Because I always had an interest in psychology, I wanted to go back to school to pursue education and a career that gave me satisfaction and one I enjoyed immersing myself in everyday. I quickly realized biology was not only a fascinating subject but one that I understood well and could apply various concepts within the subject to everyday life. Similarly, psychology classes were second nature and ones in which I could interweave biological concepts to solidify my understanding. This is what led me to actively pursue a degree which combined concepts from both fields since the observable actions from individuals were just chemicals within the brain either being released or absorbed. Along the way, through breadth courses, I discovered my keen interest in Economics and Finance. These courses helped me to better understand and navigate the world around me with concepts of business models, investing, and management.

Aspirations

I am interested in pursuing a Masters degree after graduation, either in Economics or Finance. I would like the opportunity to expand my knowledge in these fields while diving into more depth on their applications in the healthcare industry. Healthcare economics is a critical field which ties back to my aspirations of working in healthcare administration. There is a definite need for professionals who understand scientific terminology to bridge the gap in understanding the needs between various departments within the hospital floor itself to the higher level executives on the board of the hospital. At the same to be able to navigate budgets for hospitals, research and grants availability to keep the hospital on the leading edge of medical advancements, and overall day to day healthcare costs, making strides in positively impacting patient outcomes.
